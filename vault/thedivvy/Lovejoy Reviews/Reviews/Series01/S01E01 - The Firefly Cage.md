##  The Firefly Cage

## Plot Summary


## Main Review

"Who will start me for a hundred?   Don't lose it for another fifty.  Lovejoy!"

And we are off.  Episode 1, Series 1.  Watching this now—over 40 years later—and I would still be hooked. What a start.

Dilapidated Volvo, Huey Lewis and the News, Power of Love floating over the Suffolk countryside.  Lovejoy emerges from smoking Volvo (a modern-day antique car) into a bucolic scene with a slightly dishevelled, thatched cottage surrounded at the end of a farm lane we are led to believe is his gaff.

Cut to Eric in a market town square, listening to hair metal on a quaint old tape/walkman type thing (think Walkman was too pricey for my family so we had the Saisho/Matsui equivalent - hopefully Eric does as well). Turns out his butcher dad is prepared to pay this Lovejoy to train hapless drifting vegan Eric in the way of the divvy.  

Gimbert is delivered up next — cigar in mouth and sly smile.  We have our villain and Lovejoy's nemesis slash landlord.  Not quite Joffrey Baratheon; more a Tom to Lovejoy’s Jerry. It is implied later that Jerry has deflowered Tom's sister. Not sure Looney Tunes covered this.

"If home is where the heart is; then this is home to me" says Lovejoy to the camera in the antiques auction house. The fourth wall collapses as quickly as Spurs’ midfield. 

Ah fack -  now we have Dandy Jack. Three big hitters end the run with a soft 4th.  The name suggests a crazy bon vivant.  Instead, we get a posh bank manager type who appears half-heartedly in a lot of series 1 episodes and then, after the five-year hiatus between series 1 and 2, is dropped and never mentioned again.  Lovejoy’s universe answers to Jar Jar Binks. Dandy will be back.  Offering Jack.

Tinker up next.  One of the big 5.  Only minutes in and the main cast are mainly with us and they seem fully formed already.   Tinker explains what a barker is to Eric (lesson number 1, the lessons dry up after this tidbit - I was going to measure but nowt worth measuring) and that he is one (alcoholic, sniffs out antiques) and we now have our holy triumvirate.  A divvy, a barker and a City & Guilds student.  Spot.  On.

Lot 29.  What turns out to be the Bamboo Japanese Firefly Cage.  The other bidders are throwing their arms around like they’re signalling for a plane to land.  Not Lovejoy.  Just the most subtle of left (always left) half-closed eye winks, and his bid is accepted.  Unfortunately, the police arrive mid-bidding to arrest Lovejoy - and no one seems surprised.  A loveable rogue.  The lady who was bidding against Lovejoy wins.  Now I remembered this lady as being played by Lysette Anthony; but it isn't (Kim Thomson), although Lysette does appear in a later series 1 episode where Lovejoy spends a fair portion of the runtime persuading the girl to allow him to prove he is definitely not 'deadwood'.  And I know in the books Lovejoy is mid-20s but a) in the tv series Shane is in his 40s and b) very few episodes are based on the books and c) Lovejoys conquests seem a  lot younger than him - until Joanne Lumley's age appropriate Victoria Cavero turns up in later episodes!

Lovejoy at the police station - “I deal in antiques, don't steal them - you know that" response to a theft accusation seems to hold as much water as Prince Andrew’s lack of sweating  and Slough Pizza Hut claims, but it seems the police don't have much to go on, so he can leave to chase the mysterious woman and her bamboo as she hares off in a mustard Citroën 2CV.  Still only 10 minutes in, and the script, the acting, the scenery are as masterfully curated as a Fabergé egg.

Lovejoy and Eric meet for the first time.  Brief parlez, and Lovejoy is in Eric's sidecar chasing the bamboo lady. Few laps around a rural crossroads and through a forge (stupid spot to do a dedication), and we have our first glimpse of Lady Jane (Felsham) as she and some posher Grange hill candidates gets splattered.  Seems incredible as the series goes on, Lovejoy and Jane have never met before.  

The water obstacle must have had some transformative effect on the space/time continuum; in the next scene, Eric is pushing a bike with Lovejoy in a sidecar, and it is dark, and they stumble across a theft in a house Lovejoy knows should be empty of humans.  

Anyway (and a heads up going forward, most reviews won't be this detailed, more sort of emotion/general feeling - but the first episode is a pure delight and it is...the first episode!), we are now in Lovejoy’s barn cum workshop, and this place looks great.  Bric-a-brac all over the place. Tea cups, paper.  It is what I think of when I think of Lovejoy.  He will move out of this particular barn after series 1, but the series 2 barn looks much the same.  The cynic in me wonders if it was the same set.

From here we are following the lump of coal/Bluetooth speaker to the woman whose dead husband carved it (who is also the mother of Nichola) to a JCB dealer who also has a link as they all search for the clue to some treasure.  Honestly - I have watched this episode three times recently and the storyline is as labyrinthine as Ulysses.  Takes a genius to truly fathom where we are.  I am not he.
