## One Wink Plot

One antique dresser, one secret drawer and one recently released psychopath. What could possibly go wrong? Also, somehow, we're back on a boat for a budget East Anglian version of Captain Phillips.

## Review

> "You'd better run, you'd better hide...  
> Here come the twentieth century's latest scam..."

This episode starts brilliantly, then fades a little as it heads into London and into the kind of stereotypes (French, Welsh, Jewish — all bases covered) not seen on TV since Alf Garnett was dropped.

After Lovejoy hides the antique Welsh dresser (it looks horrific) from other bidders at the auction, we get the fantastic auctioneer muttering the famous lines heard in the opening sequence: 'At three fifty. Lovejoy!' So it was the dresser Lovejoy won!

The opening scenes are a delight. A car chase along country lanes as Lovejoy tries to outrun what turns out to be a tax collector, and then from the market square into Dandy Jack's antique shop to hide out (this is all Dandy does in the episode), and onto the café. The tax lady catches up — Lovejoy owes £10k!

The Welsh dresser, it transpires, contains an Arabian headdress with gold ducats on it.
