## One Wink Plot

A nervous client wants Lovejoy to authenticate a piece of Japanese lacquerware, which immediately gets stolen, which immediately involves a dead body, which immediately involves Lovejoy drinking Champagne in an aristos attic and then culminates in a whisk soaked boat chase..for some reason.

## Review

From the moment the opening credits start — the music, the bucolic pictures, the coolness of Ian McShane — I was hooked.

We open on metal filings falling from a small brass object. Then the camera pulls back and we get the reveal: Lovejoy himself, followed by the title card, *based on the books by Jonathan Gash* — and broken glass.

It's understated, but majestic. Like a duck landing.

Straight into Lovejoy at the wheel, old Volvo smoking away, completely at ease. Huey Lewis and the News playing. Around him: barns, fields, a beautiful thatched cottage that looks like it belongs on a postcard. Which, as we later find out, it sort of does — his "borrowed" living situation a nod to disenfranchised 2020s Britain. Ahead of his time.

We're then straight into Eric — headphones on, blasting something hair-metal 80s (what is that, Pantera? Megadeth?), completely disconnected from everything around him. His butcher dad despairing of his vegetarian son, and a quick Thatcher jab just to make sure we know it's the mid 80s.

And then, just over four minutes in, we get the first proper Lovejoy moment.

He turns to camera:

> "If home is where the heart is… then this is home for me."

Standing in an antiques fair.

That's it — four and a half minutes in:

- fourth wall broken
- character established
- entire premise laid out

No build-up, no explanation. Just confidence.

This divvy is in.

## Guest Star Watch / Divvy Observation

**Guest Stars:** Kim Thomson, Ronald Fraser & Sarah Lawson shine in this opening episode.
